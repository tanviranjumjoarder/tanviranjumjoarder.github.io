01 · ABOUT

A bridge from engineering to evolutionary genomics.

I spent four years engineering national-scale consumer-data systems at the Bangladesh Rural Electrification Board — designing the analytics behind electricity consumption, billing, load management, and GIS for tens of millions of customers across a 5.5-GW grid.

That work taught me something the textbooks don't: the hardest problems in machine learning don't live in benchmark datasets. They live where data is messy, biased, partial, and politically loaded. Genomics is exactly that kind of problem.

Starting Fall 2026, I'm joining Prof. Michael DeGiorgio's group at Florida Atlantic University to work on convolutional, transfer, and tensor-based learning for detecting selective sweeps — with particular focus on extending these methods to the world's most under-represented populations.

02 · RESEARCH

What I think about
at the bench.

Machine-learning methods that read evolution out of genomic data — built for the populations the field was not designed around.

⬡

Detecting natural selection with deep learning.

Convolutional and tensor-based neural networks that classify selective sweeps — hard, soft, balancing, and adaptive introgression — directly from haplotype matrices treated as images. Building on the SKINET, α-DAWG, and TrIdent lineage of methods.

cnnselective-sweepsimage-basedtensor-decomposition
⇄

Transfer learning across populations.

Closing the gap between simulated training data and real-world populations that look nothing like the simulations.

transfer-learningdomain-adaptation
