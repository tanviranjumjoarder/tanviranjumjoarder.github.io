01 / Biography

About the researcher

Tanvir Anjum Joarder is an incoming doctoral researcher in the DeGiorgio Lab at the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Florida Atlantic University. He joins the program in Fall 2026 to pursue a PhD in Computer Science with a research focus on statistical and machine learning methods for population genomic inference.

He received his B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Rajshahi University of Engineering and Technology (RUET), Bangladesh, in 2019. His undergraduate thesis, supervised by Professor A. H. M. Sarowar Sattar, investigated supervised classification for early detection of hepatocellular carcinoma from longitudinal online search-query patterns, and was published at IEEE EICT 2019. He achieved the maximum GPA of 5.00 in both his Higher Secondary (2014) and Secondary School (2012) certificates.

Since January 2022 he has served as Assistant General Manager (IT) at the Bangladesh Rural Electrification Board, leading machine learning for unit-sales prediction and system-loss forecasting, BERC-tariff billing and anomaly detection, ArcGIS mapping of the 33 kV / 11 kV / 6.35 kV network for SCADA readiness, and transformer load, complaint and website systems for an organisation serving more than 80% of the population of Bangladesh.

His research interests include convolutional and tensor-based deep learning for selective-sweep detection, transfer learning across demographic histories, statistical inference of natural selection, and machine learning for genomic populations historically underrepresented in published studies — with particular focus on South Asian genomic diversity. In parallel he conducts applied research on ML for the power-distribution sector.

02 / Research Interests

Areas of inquiry

Methodological research spanning machine learning, computational evolutionary genomics, and applied data science for healthcare and the power-distribution sector.

01

Machine Learning

Supervised, unsupervised and representation learning that transfers across domains — from genomes to national-scale grid telemetry.

02

Deep Learning

Convolutional and tensor-based architectures that treat structured scientific signals as multi-channel images for inference.

03

Data Science

Turning large, messy, real-world datasets — billing, sensor and genomic — into reproducible, decision-grade insight.

04

Population Genomics

Detecting natural selection and reconstructing demographic history, with emphasis on underrepresented populations.

05

AI for Healthcare

Machine learning over noisy, temporally structured biomedical signals, including early disease detection from behavioural data.

06

Cybersecurity

Anomaly and fraud detection on infrastructure data, robustness under adversarial behaviour, and security for utility systems.

07

Explainable AI

Interpretable, trustworthy models for high-stakes scientific and infrastructure decisions where the 'why' matters.

Active research directions

Current methodological threads

R1

Deep Learning for Selection Detection

CNN and tensor-based architectures trained on coalescent simulations to classify selective sweeps from raw haplotype data treated as multi-channel images.

R2

Transfer Learning Across Demographies

Domain adaptation that closes the simulation-to-reality gap, letting models generalise to novel populations and divergent evolutionary histories.

R3

South Asian Population Genomics

Extending selection inference and demographic modelling to South Asian populations — a quarter of humanity, yet underrepresented in genome data.

R4

Statistical Genetics & Coalescent Theory

Likelihood and summary-statistic scans for selection (SweepFinder, BALLET, VolcanoFinder) and the theory behind null comparisons for ML.

R5

Reproducible Bioinformatics

Workflow engineering with Snakemake and Nextflow, containerised software, and HPC scheduling with Slurm for reusable methods.

R6

Machine Learning for Biomedical Signal

Supervised classification of hepatocellular carcinoma from longitudinal search-query data — ML on noisy, temporal biomedical signals.

R7

EV Charging Infrastructure

Modelling EV charging demand at the distribution level, optimal station siting, and impact of large-scale EV adoption on rural networks.

R8

Electricity Theft Detection

Anomaly detection on smart-meter profiles to identify non-technical losses, robust under class imbalance and adversarial behaviour.

R9

Smart Grid Analytics

Operational analytics over national-scale billing, transformer load and complaint data, with load forecasting and GIS spatial analytics.
