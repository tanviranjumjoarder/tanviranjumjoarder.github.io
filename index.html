01 / Biography

About the researcher

Tanvir Anjum Joarder is an incoming doctoral researcher in the DeGiorgio Lab at the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Florida Atlantic University. He joins the program in Fall 2026 to pursue a PhD in Computer Science with a research focus on statistical and machine learning methods for population genomic inference.

He received his Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering from Rajshahi University of Engineering and Technology (RUET), Bangladesh, in 2019. His undergraduate thesis, supervised by Professor A. H. M. Sarowar Sattar, investigated supervised classification techniques for the early detection of hepatocellular carcinoma from longitudinal online search query patterns, and was published at the 4th IEEE International Conference on Electrical, Information and Communication Technology in 2019. He completed his Higher Secondary Certificate in 2014 at Chuadanga Government College and his Secondary School Certificate in 2012 at V. J. Government Secondary School, Chuadanga, with the maximum grade point average of 5.00 in both examinations.

Prior to commencing doctoral study, he has served since January 30, 2022 as Assistant General Manager (IT) Bangladesh Rural Electrification Board. In this capacity he leads the implementation of machine learning algorithms for monthly electricity unit sales prediction and system loss forecasting, manages BERC tariff based billing and anomaly detection over the consumer billing database, oversees ArcGIS based geographic information systems covering the 33 kV, 11 kV, and 6.35 kV distribution network for SCADA readiness, and administers transformer load management, complaint registration, and website operations for an organisation that collectively serves more than 80 percent of the population of Bangladesh.

His current research interests include convolutional and tensor based deep learning architectures for selective sweep detection, transfer learning across demographic histories, statistical inference of natural selection, and the application of contemporary machine learning methods to genomic populations historically underrepresented in published genomic studies, with particular focus on South Asian genomic diversity.

In parallel with his work in computational genomics, he conducts applied research on machine learning for the power distribution sector, including electric vehicle charging demand modelling, electricity theft detection from smart meter data, short term load forecasting, and spatial analytics over the geographic information layer of the national distribution network.

02 / Research Interests

Areas of inquiry

Methodological research spanning computational evolutionary genomics and applied machine learning for the power distribution sector.

R1

Deep Learning for Selection Detection

Convolutional and tensor based neural architectures trained on coalescent simulations to classify selective sweeps (hard, soft, balancing, and adaptive introgression) from raw haplotype data treated as multi channel image representations.

R2

Transfer Learning Across Demographic Histories

Domain adaptation methods that close the simulation to reality gap for population genomic inference, enabling models trained on one demographic backdrop to generalise to novel populations and divergent evolutionary histories.

R3

South Asian Population Genomics

Methodological extensions of selection inference and demographic modelling to South Asian populations, which constitute approximately one quarter of the world's population yet remain substantially underrepresented in published genome data.

R4

Statistical Genetics and Coalescent Theory

Likelihood based and summary statistic scans for natural selection (SweepFinder, BALLET, VolcanoFinder family), and the underlying coalescent and site frequency spectrum theory that supplies the null comparisons for machine learning methods.

R5

Reproducible Bioinformatics

Workflow engineering with Snakemake and Nextflow, containerised software, and HPC scheduling with Slurm. Emphasis on producing reusable, well documented, and reproducible methodological implementations.

R6

Machine Learning for Biomedical Signal

Supervised classification of hepatocellular carcinoma from longitudinal search query data, demonstrating the applicability of standard machine learning methods to noisy and temporally structured biomedical signals.

R7

Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure

Data driven modelling of EV charging demand at the distribution level, optimal siting of charging stations, and impact assessment of large scale EV adoption on rural distribution networks of Bangladesh.

R8

Electricity Theft Detection

Anomaly detection and supervised classification methods on smart meter consumption profiles to identify non technical losses, with emphasis on robust performance under class imbalance and adversarial consumer behaviour.

R9

Smart Grid Analytics

Operational analytics over national scale consumer billing, transformer load management, and complaint registration data, including short term load forecasting and spatial analytics over the geographic information layer of the distribution network.

05 / Selected Projects

Engineering and research projects

Research · 2019

Liver Cancer Detection from Online Search Logs

Designed an end to end supervised learning pipeline that curated symptom query datasets temporally aligned with disease progression stages, engineered features capturing temporal query patterns, and benchmarked six classifiers including SVM (94.30 percent), Random Forest (97.50 percent), Decision Tree (95.80 percent), Neural Network (93.50 percent), Naive Bayes (90.70 percent), and k Nearest Neighbour (85.50 percent). Published at IEEE EICT 2019.
