01 / about

A bridge from engineering to evolutionary genomics

I spent four years engineering national-scale consumer-data systems at the Bangladesh Rural Electrification Board — designing the analytics behind electricity consumption, billing, load management, and GIS for tens of millions of customers across a 5.5-GW grid.

That work taught me something the textbooks didn’t: the hardest problems in machine learning don’t live in benchmark datasets. They live where data is messy, biased, partial, and politically loaded. Genomics is exactly that kind of problem. The signatures of natural selection in a human genome are buried under demographic history, recombination, sequencing error, and decades of sampling bias.

Starting Fall 2026, I'll be joining Prof. Michael DeGiorgio’s group at Florida Atlantic University to work on convolutional, transfer, and tensor-based learning methods for detecting selective sweeps, with a particular focus on extending these methods to the world’s most under-represented populations.

02 / research

Research interests

Machine learning methods that read evolution out of genomic data — with methods that work on the populations the field was not built around.

⬡

Detecting natural selection

Convolutional and tensor-based neural networks that classify selective sweeps — hard, soft, balancing, and adaptive introgression — from haplotype matrices treated as images. Building on the SKINET / α-DAWG / TrIdent lineage of methods.

CNNselective-sweepsimage-based
⇄

Transfer learning across populations

Most ML selection-detectors are trained on simulations of one demographic history and deployed on real data that looks nothing like those simulations. I’m interested in transfer-learning architectures that close that gap — and make method development on under-represented populations tractable.

transfer-learningdomain-adaptationdeep-learning
∮

South Asian population genomics

South Asians are ~25% of humanity and a single-digit percentage of the world’s published genome data. I want to push methods, data, and questions toward the populations that have been waiting at the back of the queue — starting with the metabolic-disease-relevant variants of the Bengali genome.

South-Asiahealth-equitydemographic-inference
∑

Statistical genetics

Coalescent theory, summary-statistic-based scans (SFS, π, Tajima’s D, iHS, XP-EHH, H12), and the classical likelihood scanners (SweepFinder2, BALLET, VolcanoFinder) that remain the field’s gold-standard null comparisons.

coalescentlikelihoodSFS
▣

Reproducible bioinformatics

Snakemake / Nextflow pipelines, Slurm-scheduled HPC training jobs, containerised software, and the unglamorous engineering work that turns a method paper into a tool other people can actually run.

ML for biomedical signal

Earlier work on detecting liver cancer from search-engine query patterns (IEEE EICT 2019). The methodological prototype that convinced me the same toolkit applies to messy, real-world biomedical data — and to genomics.
